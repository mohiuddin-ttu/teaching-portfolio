Profile

Teacher-Scholar Identity

I am an applied economist whose teaching connects economic intuition, formal modeling, empirical evidence, and policy relevance. My instructional goal is to help students move beyond memorizing diagrams or formulas toward understanding how economists reason about real-world decisions, shocks, constraints, and tradeoffs.

My teaching draws on my research in oil-price shocks, consumption smoothing, climate-related labor-market adjustment, Medicare eligibility, and AI-induced trade concentration. Across courses, I emphasize analytical discipline, reproducible evidence, and clear communication.

Teaching Philosophy

Conceptual clarity first, technical rigor second, application always.

1. Build intuition

I begin with economic questions students recognize: inflation, unemployment, energy prices, market power, household budgets, and public policy.

2. Formalize the model

After intuition is established, I introduce models, assumptions, comparative statics, and empirical implications.

3. Apply with data

Students learn to interpret evidence, evaluate policy claims, and connect theory to observed economic outcomes.

Teaching Effectiveness

Assessment and Student Learning

I evaluate teaching effectiveness through student performance, classroom engagement, office-hour interactions, exam diagnostics, and feedback on the clarity of explanations. My goal is to identify where students struggle conceptually and redesign examples, assignments, and lectures accordingly.

  • Use applied examples to improve comprehension of abstract models.
  • Break complex problems into intuition, graph, equation, and interpretation.
  • Encourage students to explain economic mechanisms in words, not only solve equations.
  • Connect course topics to current policy debates and empirical evidence.
