Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in conceptual clarity, analytical discipline, and applied problem-solving. I begin with intuitive economic questions, introduce formal models carefully, and then connect those models to evidence and policy applications.

Build intuition

Start with concrete economic questions such as inflation, unemployment, prices, and household decisions.

Formalize the model

Translate intuition into assumptions, graphs, equations, comparative statics, and testable predictions.

Apply with evidence

Use real data, policy examples, and empirical reasoning to help students interpret economic outcomes.
