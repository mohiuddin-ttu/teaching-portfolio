I am a Ph.D. Candidate (ABD) in STEM-designated Economics at Texas Tech University, specializing in macroeconomics, applied econometrics, and structural quantitative modeling. My research studies how households, firms, and regions adjust to energy, climate, health, and technological shocks.

Job Market Paper

Consumer Demand and Household Welfare under Large Energy Price Shocks: Evidence and Structural Interpretation

Macroeconomics · Energy Economics · Consumption Dynamics

This paper examines whether consumption smoothing, a central implication of the Life-Cycle/Permanent-Income Hypothesis, remains stable during large oil-price episodes. Using U.S. data and an empirical framework combining IV-GMM, local projections, and Bayesian VAR analysis, the evidence indicates that consumption dynamics are strongly state-dependent.

Medicare Eligibility and Household Financial Outcomes

Uses a fuzzy regression discontinuity design around age 65 to estimate the effect of Medicare eligibility on utilization, out-of-pocket spending, debt, labor supply, and retirement.
